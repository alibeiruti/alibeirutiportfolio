When Pharmaceutical Samples Turn Into a Lifeline: How Patients Access, Collaborate, and Rely on Social Support to Overcome Gaps in HIV Medication

With the assistance of scientific breakthroughs, HIV has been changed from a deadly disease to a chronic condition which is manageable. These innovations remain insufficient for individuals facing housing instability, insurance gaps, and administrative delays. Even brief interruptions in antiretroviral therapy are associated with viral rebound, worse clinical outcomes, and increased healthcare utilization. Temporary access to medication combined with coordinated social support can serve as a critical bridge to continuity of HIV care.
